## Not just one photo. Eight ways to see yourself.

A single AI-generated portrait is a one-liner. A coherent set of six photos is a story. SceneSelf was built around eight storyline types — each one tells a different kind of story about you. Here's the quick map.

### 1. Journey — a day somewhere else

"A day in Dubai." "Tokyo night walk." "Christmas market in Vienna." One imagined trip, six different moments along the way — arrival, mid-day, golden-hour, the small details. The set reads like a playful phone dump from that day.

### 2. Ownership Flex — the weekend you keep posting about

"My weekend with a Lamborghini." "Private yacht in Monaco." Aspirational, fun, and clearly playful. The set tells a small, contained story of one object/experience, shot from a few candid angles by an imaginary friend.

### 3. Fantasy Role — the character you've always wanted to be

"Travel back as an ancient general." "Become Batman for one night." "Step into a Studio Ghibli world." Six beats of you living the role — costume locked, scenes varied, identity intact.

### 4. Milestone Event — the big moment you're working toward

"Opening my first cafe." "Defending my thesis." "Running my first marathon." Use the set as a vision board. Make it real on the screen first, then in life.

### 5. Profession — a day in the role

"A chef earning praise from a Michelin master." "A surgeon after a long operation." "An architect presenting to the board." The set captures the rhythm of a workday — the focused moments, the quiet ones, the win at the end.

### 6. Lifestyle — the version of you that lives well

"Soft life morning." "Slow Sunday with books." "Trail running before sunrise." The story is about texture, not events. Six frames of one mood.

### 7. Seasonal — the time of year that means something

"Christmas market evening." "Autumn forest walk." "Sakura season in Kyoto." Time-of-year scenes deliver instantly recognizable color and warmth.

### 8. Transformation — before and after, side by side

"From hobbyist to chef." "Bookworm to public speaker." The set is split into two halves: who you were and who you're becoming. Six beats that arc from one to the other.

## How to pick

Browse the eight clusters on the landing page. Tap "Try this" on the prompt that catches your eye. Or skip the menu and write your own one-liner — SceneSelf classifies it into the right cluster automatically.

The goal isn't to fake a life. It's to test-drive one. Most people walk away with a photo set that makes the next real version of themselves feel a little closer.
